The Jargon Tokenization Problem
How domain-specific finance terms fragment into weak tokens — and why your RAG pipeline suffers for it.

Key insight: Jargon like rehypothecation splits into tokens like ["re","hyp","oth","ec","ation"] — none of which carry the full financial meaning. Common words like bank or risk stay intact, giving them far stronger semantic anchoring in the embedding space.
Jargon vs Plain English
// same concept, very different tokenization depth
Takeaway: Plain English terms tokenize into whole, meaningful words. Jargon terms splinter into sub-word fragments that appear across unrelated contexts during training — weakening domain specificity.

Why this happens: When jargon tokens are weak, query embeddings are less stable. Small shifts in context window or softmax sampling change which docs rank in top-k — producing non-deterministic retrieval for the exact same query.
